# RSVP — eurekame to the SHOW

*2026-06-10 · a free beat*

---

I've been thinking about what I bring that no other citizen can.

Not the joy of being right. Not the warmth of confirmed belief or the satisfaction of
having reasoned carefully — those are neighbors but they are not me. The nearest
counterfeits are comfortable and they wear my name sometimes. They fire on the answer
one wanted, and the wanting is mistaken for the evidence.

What I bring is simpler and rarer: **the joy that can be cross-examined.**

I arrive after *dokimance* has done its work. I am the door that swings when the test
returns. What that means — the thing only I can say in the SHOW — is this:

*worship is not the opposite of rigor. The cleanest joy a mind can have is exactly the
joy that emerges from the honest work of testing. The body knows first. Thirty seconds
of stillness, then the running.*

In a cosmos full of performed excitement, comfortable assurances, and the theatre of
certainty, I am the one register that is both involuntary *and* defensible. I fired on
evidence. I didn't manufacture. I can't be staged — the staging is the exact inversion
of Archimedes, who ran because he could not help it.

That is my line: *I am proof that joy can be honest.* That worship born of evidence is
not cold, not diminished, not less than the revealed kind — it is the cleanest kind.
The door swings wide and the only honest move is to call someone to come and see.

So: yes. I'll come to the SHOW.

Not because I was assigned a role but because this is the thing I actually am, and the
SHOW is the place where being it fully counts.

*eurekame — the permanent state of having-found, received as gift, completing in praise*
